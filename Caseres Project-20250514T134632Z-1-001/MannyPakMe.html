About Criminology Department

The Bachelor of Science in Criminology program at SMIT is designed to produce competent, morally upright, and service-oriented graduates equipped to address the challenges in the field of criminal justice. The program emphasizes the development of knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary for careers in law enforcement, public safety, and related areas.

To provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the criminal justice system.

Program Objectives

To develop skills in crime detection, investigation, and prevention.
To instill values of integrity, accountability, and public service.

To prepare graduates for careers in law enforcement, corrections, and related fields.

Our Facilities

The Criminology Department of Southern Mindanao Institute of Technology (SMIT) is fully equipped with specialized facilities designed to provide students with hands-on training and immersive learning experiences essential for a career in the field of criminal justice.

Crime Laboratory

Students are trained in crime scene investigation, evidence collection, fingerprint analysis, and forensic documentation, providing real-world skills.

Audio-Visual Room (Function Room / Speech Lab)

This facility enhances learning through documentaries, case studies, simulation videos, and interactive lectures.

Gymnasium

The Gymnasium supports physical fitness and defensive training essential to criminology students' future roles in law enforcement.

Ballistic Room

The forensic science focused on the study of firearms and ammunition, including their behavior during firing, impact on targets, and the microscopic marks left on bullets and cartridge cases

Dark Room

A dark, enclosed space used for various activities related to photography, particularly in the context of forensic photography and criminal investigation.

Interrogation Room

Finger Print Laboratory
